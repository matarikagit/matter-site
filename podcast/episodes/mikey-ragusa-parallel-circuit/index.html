About the episode

Specific stories can travel farther than general ones.

Guest

Mikey Ragusa

Mikey Ragusa is an actor, writer, and filmmaker creating intimate, character-driven stories about queer identity, memory, and human connection. In this conversation, he talks about writing from instinct, acting in his own work, and the momentum that followed Parallel Circuit.

Instagram TikTok IMDb

Project focus

Parallel Circuit

Parallel Circuit follows two childhood best friends reuniting over winter break as unspoken feelings surface between them. Mikey wrote and acted in the short, which reached a major organic audience on YouTube with no promotion budget.

The episode digs into the difference between forcing a trend and recognizing when a real community has found the work. Mikey and Charlie discuss how the Byler community connected with the film, why specificity can become a distribution advantage, and what happens after views turn into momentum.

  • Writing from lived feeling without turning the story into autobiography.
  • Making intimate work on microbudgets without waiting for ideal resources.
  • Choosing online release instead of holding the film for a festival-first strategy.
  • Translating audience traction into producing, financing, and feature conversations.
  • Continuing the momentum with Train Tracks and the feature version of Parallel Circuit.
