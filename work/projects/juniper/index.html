Synopsis

Juniper and Cherry move through grief, danger, and the possibility of family.

After the death of his mother, Juniper is pulled into a cross-country road trip with Cherry, a former drug dealer trying to outrun her past. As the road stretches farther from home, buried trauma and unspoken truths surface, testing the limits of their connection.

Tone

Grounded. Human. Strange.

Indie film realism shaped through a quiet, existential animated lens.

World

New York to California.

A present-day American road trip where each stop reveals more of the characters.

Visual Style

Real world, hand drawn.

Photography and simplified characters create a cinematic world filtered through vulnerability.

Themes

Grief and chosen family.

The story centers connection, identity, generational trauma, and healing.

Characters

The road is carried by four damaged people.

Juniper

Withdrawn

A young man navigating grief and connection.

Cherry

Guarded

A former drug dealer trying to outrun her past.

Lyla

Protective

Cherry’s childhood best friend, still tied to her past.

Damian

Dangerous

Cherry’s ex, whose obsession refuses to let go.
