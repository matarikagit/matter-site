Juniper

A road story about grief, identity, dangerous history, and the people who become family when home stops feeling like home.

Story

A quiet grief. A loud past. One long road west.

Logline

You can’t outrun your past. But you can choose who runs beside you.

After the death of his mother, an isolated young man joins a cross-country road trip with Cherry, a former drug dealer trying to outrun her past. The farther they travel, the harder it becomes to ignore what is following them.

Grounded like an indie film. Drawn like a memory. Built like a confession.

Visual World

Real spaces. Hand-drawn people. Childhood and adulthood colliding.

Style

Imperfect on purpose.

The characters stay simple so the world around them can feel heavy, lived-in, and emotionally real.

Tone

Human before polished.

Road trip, romance, drama, and coming-of-age energy filtered through grief.
